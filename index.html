Dr Ellie Pryor.

Researcher

I am dedicated to understanding present-day South African climate and applying this knowledge to reconstruct it’s past climate during the last glacial cycle using a multi-proxy approach. For my PhD, my work linked land-sea climate changes to Homo sapiens cultural evolution during the time interval of 100-50 thousand years ago.

For my postdoc, my research focused on determining whether shells were actually heated or cooked by humans. While this might seem straightforward, it is important, not only for understanding human behaviour, but also for other analyses. For example, oxygen isotope analysis is commonly used to reconstruct past sea surface temperatures. This method only works if the shell hasn’t been heated after it formed, because heating changes its internal chemistry. Before we can use a shell for climate reconstruction, we first need to establish whether it has preserved its original state or was once exposed to fire.
